Overview

KAED Program, developed by the American University of Beirut, Suliman S. Olayan School of Business (OSB), is a hybrid learning journey designed for forward-thinking leaders navigating an AI-centric, data-driven business landscape. By combining emotional intelligence and crisis resilience with digital governance and practical AI workplace tools, this program empowers leaders to bridge the gap between digital transformation and human-centric leadership.

The core philosophy of the program is that modern leadership thrives at the intersection and harmony between human creativity/intuition and technology.

Program Details

2 days · Beirut Campus

The 21st Century Leader

Soft Skills · Hard Results
This module focuses on the "soft" skills that yield "hard" results, showing participants how to lead with high emotional intelligence and an agile mindset in an increasingly data-driven world. Participants are introduced to many of the basic principles of human behavior and introduce intrapersonal tools and related reflective techniques as a precursor to developing the leadership capacity. Core areas of coverage aligned with this include individual differences in abilities and attitudes, perceptions, motivation, power and politics, and leadership.

1 day (2 half-days) · LIVE Online

AI in the Workplace

Practical · Hands-on · Generative AI
A practical, hands-on dive into the world of Generative AI. This online module focuses on the immediate application of AI tools to automate routine tasks, enhance creative output, and reclaim time for high-value strategic thinking. This session offers an in-depth exploration of AI applications tailored to various sectors. Participants will also focus on mitigating biases in AI systems, ensuring data security and privacy, and promoting responsible AI usage.

2 days · Beirut Campus

Navigating the Digital Shift

Strategic · Holistic · Ecosystem
Digital transformation is far more than just integrating technology into operations; it represents a comprehensive strategic shift in how organizations function and engage with stakeholders. This intensive session is designed to empower emerging leaders from different sectors to understand digital transformation as a holistic ecosystem that transcends simple technology adoption. By integrating practical frameworks—such as Digital Maturity and ADKAR—with strategic concepts like the S-Curve and the "Think Big, Start Small" philosophy.

1 day (2 half-days) · LIVE Online

The Modern Workforce

Talent · Strategy · Performance
This module presents a comprehensive overview of how to effectively develop a talent management strategy that can support organizational strategy, motivate multi-generational employees, and drive organizational performance. The session's orientation will be both analytical, in giving participants concepts and frameworks with which to think about the management of talent in organizations, and managerial, in considering the action plans and implementation approach necessary to put these practices and policies into effect and sustain them over time.

2 days · Beirut Campus

Reorienting Crisis

Resilience · Preparedness · Response
This module introduces participants to the fundamentals of crisis management from understanding how organizations anticipate, prepare for, and respond to crises. The session bridges theory and practice through interactive discussions and analytical frameworks. Participants will analyze how leading institutions have successfully identified potential disruptions and built organizational resilience through proactive transformation, while contrasting these with examples of crisis failure, where leadership, culture, and governance breakdowns led to reputational damage. The session will also equip participants with the essential knowledge, skills, and competencies necessary for communicating effective crisis preparedness, management, and resilience to external and internal stakeholders.

Program Academic Overseer

Yusuf Sidani, PhD

Professor of Leadership and Business Ethics
Dean, Suliman S. Olayan School of Business – American University of Beirut

Dr. Yusuf Sidani, who has served as associate dean of research and faculty development at OSB since March 2018, interim dean since October 2020, and is taking the role of dean effective September 1, 2021. He gained his BBA from AUB in 1985 and went on to graduate studies at Indiana University (MBA, '87) and University of Mississippi (PhD '93). He has extensive teaching and course development experience in business ethics, leadership, fraud prevention, and organizational behavior. His research includes gender and diversity in organizations. He authored Business Ethics in the Middle East (Routledge, 2018) and received the Abdul Hameed Shoman Award for Arab Researchers in 2015. Dr. Sidani has been working closely with AUB leadership to uphold OSB’s high standards, including the launch of AUB’s first fully online MBA and top rankings in QS assessments.
