The Program

Artificial intelligence (AI) is reshaping supply chain management by boosting efficiency, enhancing forecasting accuracy, enabling smarter decision-making, and strengthening operational resilience.

The AI in Supply Chain Program, developed by the American University of Beirut, Suliman S. Olayan School of Business (OSB), focuses on real-world AI applications in production, demand forecasting, inventory management, logistics optimization, and risk mitigation. Through a combination of executive-level lectures and guided, non-technical hands‑on activities, participants will develop practical literacy in how AI-driven systems shape modern supply chains and how to interpret their outputs to make informed strategic decisions.

Program Instructor

Mirna Abou Mjahed, PhD

PhD in Management (Information Systems, Supply Chain, Decision Support) – NEOMA Business School
MBA, University of Houston
Bachelor in Computer and Communication Engineering, AUB

Dr. Abou Mjahed’s academic and professional work focuses on technology-enabled supply chain decision making, supply chain collaboration, inventory management and managing uncertainty and resilience in complex supply chains. She has taught at institutions including AUB Lebanon, AUB Mediterraneo, and NEOMA Business School, covering topics such as Supply Chain Optimization, Operations Management, Managerial Decision Making, Strategic Management, and Risk Management. With over a decade of professional experience in business consulting, software development, and information systems across Beirut, Houston, and Riyadh, she brings a practical, supply-chain-driven perspective to the use of new technologies in planning, coordination, and decision support.
